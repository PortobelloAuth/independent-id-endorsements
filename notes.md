# Notes

This page is not cannonical. It is intended to capture ideas that may eventually
be reflected in the specification before they have been vetted sufficiently for
inclusion.

---

- the authorization process needs to support both positive assertions and
  negative assertions. For instance, a father should generally have access to
  his minor children's medical records. However, an abusive father might need to
  be specifically prevented from having access to his wife and children's
  records in order to prevent his using them as a means to track and continue to
  abuse his family. This means we will need to look up verification information
  for the positive assertions in the submitted digital authorization documents
  and also look for negative assertion documents applicable to the subjects of
  the authorization.
